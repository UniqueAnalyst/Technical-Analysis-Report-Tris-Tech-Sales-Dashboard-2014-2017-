The primary objective is to analyze sales trends, identify key revenue drivers, evaluate shipping preferences, track customer ordering patterns, and highlight regional performance to inform strategic business decisions.

Product Categories (Technology 35%, Furniture 33%, Office Supplies 32%)

Standard Class (Leading Ship Mode, $5,927,562.04 Revenue)

Standard Class is the most popular ship mode, likely due to its cost-effectiveness. Continue offering this as the default option for most customers.

Same Day (Least Ship Mode, $496,076.45 Revenue)

Same Day shipping is underutilized, possibly due to higher costs or lack of awareness. Promote this option for urgent needs, especially in technology and furniture categories.

Offer limited-time discounts on Same Day shipping during peak months (September to December) to increase adoption.

December (Leading Month, $1,538,619.73 Revenue)

December is the highest-revenue month, likely due to holiday shopping. Prepare early by increasing inventory for top products (Canon imageCLASS 2200, technology, and furniture).

February (Lowest Month, $240,806.38 Revenue)

February is the lowest-revenue month, possibly due to post-holiday spending fatigue.

Focus on mid-tier customers (e.g., Tracy and Steve) with personalized offers to increase order frequency during this slow period.

Conclusion

The Tris-Tech Sales Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of business performance from 2014–2017, revealing strong overall revenue ($9.89M) with clear patterns in customer behavior, product preferences, and regional performance. The analysis highlights both strengths (technology product dominance, strong West region presence) and opportunities (customer diversification, shipping method optimization, regional expansion).

The visualizations effectively communicate key metrics and relationships, allowing stakeholders to quickly identify trends and make data-driven decisions.
